More About Me

Life.

Throughout my life, I’ve lived in many different countries. I was born in South Korea, but I moved to California with my family when I was about five years old. After two years or so, we moved back again to South Korea, in Gyeonggi-do Province, just outside Seoul. When I turned eleven (and just when we were getting comfortable), we once again moved to Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, which I can proudly call it my second home. We stayed in Malaysia for more than seven years, and fortunately, that was just long enough for me to complete my secondary education with an IB diploma.

In the fall of 2014, I moved to Iowa to study at Grinnell College. Interestingly, I actually spent my freshmen year as a Bio/Chem major since that was what I initially hoped to major in when I first came to the U.S. After completing the first year, I went back to South Korea to serve in the military for 21 months. That was when I got interested in programming. Instead of immediately returning to college, I took a year off to explore the world of computers and computational sciences. For the first six months, I studied programming languages like Python and HTML/CSS/JS as well as algorithms and data structures from online courses. I then spent the next six months working for a small blockchain startup company to immerse myself into the tech industry and gained invaluable experiences.

As I returned to Grinnell College in the fall of 2018, I soon declared my major in computer science. My passion for computer science and programming has deepened over the years as I continue to polish my skills and attain new experiences. Some of the specific fields that I'm currently interested in are: autonomous driving, finance-data analytics, medical AI, and bioinformatics.
